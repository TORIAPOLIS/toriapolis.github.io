Tarot, the Minor Arcana, and Meditation

Tarot is a deck of cards traditionally consisting of 78 cards. It's popularly used for divination. Learning to read tarot cards requires an understanding of the story that plays through the cards. And like a regular deck of playing cards, there are four suits that represent the Minor Arcana: wands, cups, swords, and pentacles. Each suit has 14 cards with every card having its own qualities. With so many cards to learn and no readily available "cheat sheet" I could find, I designed these posters to guide my learning of the Minor Arcana as well as to tackle the stigma that tarot has developed through biased media representation.

Tarot and the story it tells can be widely interpreted and holds a different meaning to each of its users. With this perception, Tarot can be used to guide the user's journey through readings and to help them find their own meaning to the story. As a Tarot user myself, it guides my journey of meditation and self-reflection. By creating accessible infographics, I hope that many people will be curious to learn about Tarot beyond it's divinatory popularity.

Minor Arcana Icons

I illustrated the four suits in Illustrator sketching the main elements: wand, cup, sword, and pentacle, on paper beforehand. Once I had the right sketch, I'd combine it with the repeating elements of stars, sparkles and the cloudy wisp. These illustrations are meant to appear magical, in the sense of "power", and the user taking power into their own hands to learn a little more about themselves.
