What is gubi?

Gubi is a product packaging and branding concept for a multivitamin service. Customers order online and tailor their vitamins to their needs or pick a generic pre-made assortment of gummy multivitamins divided up by days of the week. The gummy multivitamin is attractive to young children and targets trendy mothers who are health conscious.

Developing gubi required creating an entire brand identity that would unify its assets whether for packaging, marketing, or corporate identity. It also required following one constraint for the packaging: no rectangular-shaped packaging - no boxes.

Research & Process


Building this product from concept to reality required brainstorming and devising a step-by-step plan. One of the first steps was utilizing word lists to decide on what the product was and who the product is geared towards. Afterwards, a style guide was created along with a brand identity.

The logo for gubi alludes to a toy block to represent how gubi multivitamins are building blocks for maintaining one's health. The initial logo had the block in a shape that was too long, and the plus signs in the second iteration seemed too arbitrary. Eventually the fourth icon shown (definitely not the fourth iteration) is used in the final packaging.




Packaging was developed after researching biomimicry practices and how to make packaging efficiently. Using a hexagonal shape similar to the bee's honeycomb hives can reduce the amount of packaging used. However in the current health supplement market, it is evident that using a particular plastic bottle is what's most cost-effective even if it isn't necessarily what's best for long-term sustainability practices.

For this particular prototype the packaging is entirely made of paper. If it were made in market the casings for each day's supplements would probably be made of one plastic, where the supplements arrive by mail on a monthly basis, and the user refills their supplement case with the gummy vitamins and other supplements if they so wish.
