ABOUT THE PROJECT

I entered my first design contest and it was to design a poster for the 45th Anniversary celebration of the Asian American Studies department at UC Davis. Besides that information and the date of the event, I proceeded with making the poster. In my brainstorming phase, I wanted the emphasis to be on the text "45th Anniversary". I thought that a celebratory feel with vivid colors and geometric shapes that allude to origami folding would be a good representation of the ASA department. After submitting my design, I was notified that I was a semi-finalist and that I would be competing against one other to win the contest. In order to win, I had to rework my poster design.


I titled this work as "Intersections". The professors of the department agreed that my work was compositionally pleasing but did not feel that they identified with it. They encouraged me that in my re-design that I should develop the folding shapes to better represent ASA history as one of protest and resistance. At that point I realized I did not fully understand the history of ASA. I interviewed a friend in the ASA major who gave me a crash course on how ASA started as a coalition at SF State which grew out of anger and discontent.



When I think of resistance and protest I think of solidarity and all the images of people throwing up their fists or linking arms, etc. I played with different hand arrangements and went with a rough idea of joining hands without literally having the hands joined together. The hands are instead joined by the two colored triangles in the center.
