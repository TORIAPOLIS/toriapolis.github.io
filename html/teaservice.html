Redesigning the teabag

Tea as a Service

Experience - Product

ABOUT THE PROJECT

The objective for this project was to pick a commonly used item and to re-design it with sustainability in mind. I chose to re-design the teabag and to offer the tea as a service within a small local community like Davis. My full research and life cycle assessment of tea can be read here: Rethinking and Redesigning the Disposable Teabag.


By identifying the different materials used in a disposable tea bag, I can assess which materials are not sustainable and should be removed from the final product. I noticed that most tea bags have an extensive amount of space for the tea leaves that go unused. The paper tag and string along with the stapled packaging is excessive. By removing the excessive elements and reducing the tea bag size, those resources will not have to end up at the local landfill. There is less convenience without the string, but depending on the dimensions, the bag can float in such a way that it can be pulled out.



What process does tea normally go through to get to the user? This is a chart I included in my research paper depicting the process from tea harvest in another country to eventually getting distributed to individual households. I found this process to be ineffective because of the high rate of emissions from having to do multiple forms of packaging and distribution overseas.



So I created a closed tea harvest and distrubtion cycle catering to the Davis, California community. Harvest and cultivation would take place on UC Davis land and can be utilized for research. When harvest is complete, those who've subscribed to the tea service can have their tea delivered by bike. Once the tea bags are used, they can be returned to the UC Davis facility for composting.



This poster brings together all the graphical elements I designed for my research paper. I presented it to a group of designers and community planners.
