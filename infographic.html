Utility Bill Re-design

Category

Infographic

Imagine you can't afford access to the internet to view your gas and electric bill online. You may feel the guilt of receiving paper bills, but it's your only means of knowing how much money you owe. So why not make it an experience that is easy to understand with added benefit?

As of mid 2015, the PG&E utility bill informs readers of their account information, the amount they owe, "important" messages, and an infographic of electrical/gas use over time. However the hierarchy and use of this information can be re-arranged to be more direct and useful.

Infographic change

In this diagram, it shows the use of gas and electricity in a bar chart. It can be tricky to understand as the two amenities are combined into a single bar per billing period. Once you understand the general trend of your gas and electricity usage, how can users use this data? A survey on how to improve the bill was conducted on 15-20 people. A majority of the sample group was early 20s college students. Other surveyers were working people in their late 20s to 50s. Some suggested tips on how to save money others said any sort of extra information was unneccesary and all they cared about was knowing how much money they had to pay.

With these suggestions taken into account, the previous chart was split into two to represent gas and electricity usage separately. A new added feature is the orange dashed line representing average daily use by households within a county. Based on this average, you can see how you're doing compared to other households in your area.
